Population Stability

Ecosystems provide many benefits to humanity in the form of resources, mental health benefits, a sense of place, and cultural value. However, the maintenance of these benefits depends on the health of these ecosystems and their stability through time. For this reason, monitoring the stability of ecosystems is an important objective of conservation.

Early warning signals are statistical signatures of systems such as animal populations suggested as indicators of stability. These indicators allow conservationists to predict when populations are facing local extinctions using simple abundance data. However, in research done with colleagues at the University of Zurich and the University of Bristol, we show that when these indicators are based on abundance data they are sensitive to the length and resolution of the abundance time series Arkilanian et al., 2020. We show that basing these indicators on species traits known to be influenced by environmental stress greatly reduces their sensitivity.
